FIELD NOTES

FROM THE SKETCHBOOK OF SANDRA MILLS


Featuring

  • An appreciation for the outdoors
  • Observations
  • Lessons learned
  • Tips and tricks
  • Insight into the Bird Nerd mind

A lot of species are indistinguishable from each other. Maybe early bird enthusiasts made up these differences to claim more species in the province? Conspiracy theory much?

Did some amazing spotting today and saw a common nighthawk sleeping in a tree. Such a great bird! Blob by day; rocket-boomerang by night.

We got hooked on birding in the Fall, when trees are bare, and birds are easy to spot. We miss-judged what Summer birding would hold.

One problem we run into a lot is describing a specific branch in a forest. Maybe we should get a laser pointer?

We were on a 10 game win streak when we decided to go searching for borrowing owls. After hours and hours of scanning empty fields we formed our research.

Closing thoughts

I use a Canon 7Dmkii camera and a Canon 400mm f5.6 lens. My setup is middle of the road but I'm happy with it. Does a photographer need top of the line equipment to produce great bird images? Nope, the good news is that you don't need a caribou-sized lens for birding! A DSLR and telephoto lens are nice but even a point and shoot will add a creative aspect to your adventures!
